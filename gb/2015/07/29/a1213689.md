<tr><td VALIGN=TOP width="626"><h2 align=center>宠物狗留车里 好心人制止遭谩骂</h2>

<h6></h6>
<hr>
	<p>【新唐人2015年07月28日讯】夏天，把<ahref="https://github.com/ugwazd396/ntdtv/blob/master/gb/宠物狗.md#1">宠物狗</a>狗留在车里绝对不是件理智的事儿。尤其在北美，甚至能因此获罪。不过，在加拿大温哥华由此而引发的街头争执，让人大跌眼镜。</p>
<p>在温哥华维多利亚街（6941 Victoria Dr.）的宠物店外拍摄下的一段视频，记录了一名年轻男子将他的法国斗牛犬留在车后座，只在窗子上开了个小缝隙。一位路人发现了车中的狗狗，气喘吁吁，流口水，还把鼻子奋力伸到窗外，便好心制止。结果……遭到车主的高声谩骂。</p>

<p>拍摄这段视频的宠物店员工Jennifer Thi 随后将视频发到Facebook上，并写下了事件经过。引起不少网友的关注和讨论。</p>
<p>有网友表示，他认为“窗户开的小缝”有所帮助，但是为什么不把窗户多开一些呢？</p>
<p>也有人评论，该男子明知道把<ahref="https://github.com/ugwazd396/ntdtv/blob/master/gb/宠物狗.md#1">宠物狗</a>留在车里不对。虽然每年夏季不能把宠物<ahref="https://github.com/ugwazd396/ntdtv/blob/master/gb/孩子.md#1">孩子</a>留在车里的公众教育都在做，但也许直到他的狗，因此而死，才能让他相信车里到底有多热。</p>
<p>小编建议，应该把这位狗主人送去下面这个试验，换位思考一下，他就知道开个小缝到底够不够了。</p>

<p>以下是Jennifer在Facebook上的留言。</p>
<div>
<blockquote><p>The shocking encounter is not only hostile but disturbing in regards to the man’s continued rant against the two women attempting to help his dog. Read Jennifer’s story:</p>
<p>So today at work (I work at a pet food and supplies store) this happened. Here’s the story:</p>
<p>A woman walks into my store and asks if we know whose french bulldog was in a grey car in our parking lot (we share a parking lot with a 7/11 and a hair salon). She said it was left in a car and was in distress, so I went out with her to check it out. Upon looking at the dog, I could tell it was overheating – the windows in the car were opened half an inch and the dog was panting heavily, drooling, and was trying to be as close to the crack of fresh air as possible with not even any water in the car.</p>
<p>May I also mention that french bulldogs are extremely susceptible to respiratory issues and trouble breathing?</p>
<p>As my partner is about to call the non emergency line, I’m trying to find out how to help this dog. I didn’t expect anything to happen from doing this, but I tried opening the passenger side door when I found out it was unexpectedly unlocked. We both are shocked and have no idea whose car or dog this was, so we agreed that I should bring it into my store and wait for the owner to pick it up.</p>
<p>(Note: the woman, before coming to my store, had gone into both the 7/11 and the hair salon asking for the owner of the vehicle and no one had said anything.)</p>
<p>Just as we’re going to leash the dog, a man who has been standing in front of this car THIS ENTIRE TIME sipping on a slurpee asks us what we’re doing. He has heard everything we’ve said, he’s silently watched as I’ve opened the car door and get a hold of the dog.</p>
<p>I ask him if the car is his. He says yes. The dog is his, too.</p>
<p>My partner then proceeds to tell him that he shouldn’t have left his dog in the car and that his windows were nowhere close to being rolled down sufficiently when he turns completely angry on us and tells us we know nothing about dogs or hot cars, that his dog had air through the cracks of opened window he gave it and therefore was completely comfortable, and basically yelling about us not being justified in trying to save his overheating bulldog.</p>
<p>He then turns to me, where I’m standing by the opened door making sure his dog doesn’t run into the street, and tells me to “not touch (his) fucking door and dog.” I don’t comply because 1) I’m allowing his dog to breathe and 2) by this time he was being such an asshole that I wasn’t going to give in to his orders just because he was taller and bigger and louder than me.</p>
<p>He then proceeds to march over to where I am, slam the door right in front of me (almost slamming it on his dog’s face) and forcefully walks right into me and starts screaming in my face.</p>
<p>The video starts in the latter end of his screaming act at me. The rest you can watch for yourself.</p>
<p>I’m very lucky that the woman I was with filmed this and caught his face, how much his windows were rolled down, and his licence plate. People like this shouldn’t have pets and shouldn’t get away with unacceptable behaviour.</p>
<p>It would mean a lot if you could share this to spread the word about leaving animals in hot, unventilated cars. I suppose some people just don’t get it… But maybe after seeing how irrational his argument is, hopefully some people will think twice before making a “sandwich and slurpee” stop. You can’t wait until your pet dies from being in a hot car to realize how quickly it can get hot and how a little sliver of a window essentially does nothing.</p></blockquote>
</div>
